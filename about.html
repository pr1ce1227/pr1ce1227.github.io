My Story

Hello everyone! My name is Caleb Price. I'm currently a graduate student at Brigham Young University, studying Electrical and Computer Engineering. I received my undergraduate degree from BYU in Computer Engineering. I didn’t always want to be a computer engineer or any kind of engineer, for that matter. I was born on a family farm in eastern Washington, where I spent thousands of hours laboring in the fields and driving heavy machinery. I grew up playing football, running track, and learning to play the trumpet and piano. After graduating from high school, I decided to serve a Christian mission in Cape Verde, a group of 10 islands off the west coast of Africa. I spent 2 years there, learning Portuguese and two other African dialects, while teaching the people about my faith and helping them with their daily lives.

After my service, I returned to Brigham Young University with the intent of studying business marketing. I quickly became bored and decided to return to the world of numbers. With little experience in engineering outside of working on farm equipment, I decided to enter the field of mechanical engineering. Along this journey, I met my beautiful wife, Bronte, and we have now been married for 4 years. After 2 years in mechanical engineering, I took my first electrical class, which included circuits and coding. I quickly fell in love with the subject and promptly changed my major to Computer Engineering. I have never regretted this decision and am happy that my long journey eventually led me to this field of study.

I am now pursuing my master’s degree, focusing my research on the effects of radiation on Systems on Chips in harsh environments, whether in space or military applications. I love tinkering with personal projects, as you will see throughout this website, and I am excited to use my knowledge to make this world a better place. Enjoy the projects!
