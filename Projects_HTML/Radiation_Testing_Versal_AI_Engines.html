Radiation Testing Versal AI Engines

Main Concepts

  • Radiation effects including varying LET from different ions, energies, and fluence
  • Targeting AI engines, memories, processors, networking
  • Data retrieval through UART, JTAG, PCIe
  • Collaborated with NASA and DOD
  • Basis for my thesis

Materials Used

  • VCK190 Board
  • Polarfire (PCIE)
  • Microzed 7010 (JTAG)
  • Network Controlled Power Supply
  • Network Switch
  • Laptops monitoring the logs at run time

Summary

The goal of the project was to test the Versal ACAP AI engines under varying levels of radiation in a controlled environment. This testing was conducted at Brookhaven National Laboratory with the support of NASA and the DOD. The challenge was significant due to the complexity of the Versal ACAP chip, which includes 400 AI engines, over 7 processors, and more than 8 memories, all exposed to the radiation beam.

To effectively test this chip, we needed to monitor all these components so that any failures could be traced back to their specific sources. We successfully completed the testing, allowing us to calculate cross-sections for failures in the AI engines, their memories, and the memories of the other processors. For more details, keep an eye out for my thesis and other published papers.
