Summary

In today's world you have to have a 3D printer to call yourself and engineer even if you don't make the designs yourselves as is the case with most my prints. That being said for anyone who has 3d printed, they know its not quite so simple as press play and your done, especially if you don't have the nicest printer on the market. I quickly learned how big of a difference 5 degrees celcius make when melting the filament. As well as the importance of having a consistant heat on your printing bed and a clean surface. It took me quite a while to get the hang of it especiallly with more advance print with supports and odd angles. (See the deer for a good example) Overall I have really enjoyed my printer and nothing quite makes you feel like Iron Man then having your 3d printer running while soldering up a PCB and wating for your code to compile.
