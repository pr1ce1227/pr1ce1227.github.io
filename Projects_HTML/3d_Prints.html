Summary

In today's world, you have to have a 3D printer to call yourself an engineer, even if you don't make the designs yourself, as is the case with most of my prints. That being said, anyone who has 3D printed knows it's not quite as simple as pressing play and being done, especially if you don't have the nicest printer on the market.

I quickly learned how big of a difference 5 degrees Celsius can make when melting the filament, as well as the importance of having a consistent heat on your printing bed and a clean surface. It took me quite a while to get the hang of it, especially with more advanced prints that require supports and have odd angles (see the deer for a good example). Overall, I have really enjoyed using my printer. Nothing quite makes you feel like Iron Man more than having your 3D printer running while soldering up a PCB and waiting for your code to compile.
