Radiation Testing Versal AI Engines

Main Concepts

  • Radiation effects including varying LET from different ions, energies, and fluence
  • Targeting AI engines, memories, processors, network
  • Data retrieval through UART, JTAG, PCIe
  • Collaborated with NASA and MDA
  • Basis for my thesis

Materials Used

  • VCK190 Board
  • Polarfire (PCIE)
  • Microzed 7010 (JTAG)
  • Network Controlled Power Supply
  • Network Switch
  • Laptops monitoring the logs at run time

Summary

The Goal of the project was to test the Versal Acap AI engines under varrying LET's in a controlled radiation environment. This was accomplished at Brookhaven National Labratory with the help of NASA and the MDA. The challenge with this project is the Versal ACAP chip is a very complicated including 400 AI engines, 7+ processors, 8+ memories. All of which are being hit by the radiation beam.

To test this chip we needed to be able to monitor all these different components, so that when something failed we could trace it back to its relevant source. We were able to successfully test this board and calculate cross sections for the AI engines failure, their memories and memories of all the other processors. Keep an eye out for my thesis and other published papers for more of the details.
