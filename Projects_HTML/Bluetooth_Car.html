Bluetooth Low Energy Car

Main Concepts

  • Bluetooth Low Energy
  • Python
  • Raspberry PI Programming
  • Platform IO (VS Code)
  • Stepper motor controls

Materials Used

  • Raspberry Pi Pico W (Microcontroller on car)
  • Raspberry PI 4b (Powering touchscreen)
  • H-Brigde Power distribuiton
  • 2 DC Motors
  • Custom designed platform and steering mechanism
  • Stepper Motor

Summary

Bluetooth has always been something I wanted to experiment with, and this car was my chance. I wanted to step away from Arduino and try something new. This decision led me to the Raspberry Pi market, and I really enjoyed the switch. Having a full operating system on such a small device while still having easy access to the GPIO opened up a whole bunch of possibilities.

In this project, I mainly focused on using Bluetooth Low Energy with a couple of GPIOs to handle the car's controls. I used a stepper motor to drive the steering mechanism that I designed, allowing for much smoother steering and better control of the vehicle.

The project, as always, proved to be more challenging than I expected. Just designing the car and printing everything took over 10 hours. After that, I began messing around with the Bluetooth. While I was comfortable with byte manipulation in C and C++, it took me a moment to understand how to do this in Python. I also had the fun challenge of mixing an operating system with a bare-metal system. I was pretty happy with the end product but learned why remote-controlled cars use radio frequency and not Bluetooth, as the car ran out of range at about 30 feet.
